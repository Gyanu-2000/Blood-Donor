Before you donate

  • Eat and drink:

    Have a healthy meal within a few hours of your appointment, focusing on iron-rich foods, and drink plenty of water or sports drinks leading up to it.

  • Get rest:

    Ensure you get a good night's sleep the night before.

  • Bring ID:

    Bring your ID or driver's license for identification.

  • Fill out paperwork:

    You will complete a health questionnaire and a private screening that includes a mini-physical to check your blood pressure, temperature, and hemoglobin levels.

  • Check eligibility:

    There are general guidelines for eligibility, such as age (often 17 or 16 with consent), weight (at least 110 pounds), and overall health.

During the donation

  • The process:

    • Relax for about 15 minutes in the refreshment area, where you will be offered a snack and a drink.

  • Length of appointment:

    • The entire experience typically takes about an hour, including registration, the screening, the donation, and the recovery period.

  • Frequency:

    • You can donate whole blood approximately every 8 weeks (56 days).

After you donate

  • Rest and recover:

    • Relax for about 15 minutes in the refreshment area, where you will be offered a snack and a drink.

  • Follow instructions:

    • A bandage will be placed on your arm, and you will receive instructions to follow.

  • Avoid certain activities:

    • For a few hours after donating, avoid heavy lifting, smoking, and strenuous activity.
